Unemployment rate is a big deal to all of us. As we are nearing graduation, we are looking for a job. That is why for our project we decided to explore datasets revolving unemployment rates. Out of few of the main countries we explored the highest and lowest unemployment rates. Looking at our first visualization you can see that Brazil has the highest unemployment rate of 12.54 %. Also, from that visualization you can see that the United States is a country that has one of the lower unemployment rates.


As we live in the United States, we decided to explore unemployment rates in just the United States. We decided to look at unemployment rates over the period 1948-2019. This would better help us understand what the unemployment rate will look like in the future by looking at the trends in the past. As you can see looking at the visualization there is a recession every 5 to 10 years. Also looking at the visualization you can see that the unemployment rate in 2019 is at its lowest which is around 3.6 percent. We were very happy to find out that the unemployment rate is at one of its lowest points since we are currently applying for jobs.

We then wanted to go more in depth and look at the employment to population ratio of workers ages 25-54 from 1989 to 2019. This would give us a more in depth understanding of what our chances would be like getting a job. Looking at our next visualization you can see that the employment to population ratio is at one of its highest which is around 80 percent.

Moving further along we thought to ourselves what salary would be like for different careers. That is why we found data on 22 different major categories of occupations for the 50 different states. In California the average annual salary for computer and mathematical occupations is 95.32k which is a highly motivating factor for us to get a job as soon as possible.

Lastly, we wanted to focus on the general happiness of people that are unemployed vs employed. Looking at our last visualization you can see that unemployed people are generally unhappier than those that are employed. To add extra motivation to searching for a career, we can look at this visualization to show that individuals that are unemployed are unhappy, so we need to get a job to be happy.
